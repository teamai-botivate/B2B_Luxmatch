§ 01  ·  Overview
Two doors, one house.
LuxeMatch serves shoppers and jewellers from the same domain. The visual language, performance budget, and trust posture are shared; the entry points and dashboards are not.
The shopper
A first-time visitor or returning browser. Likely on an iPhone or mid-range Android during commute, lunch, or evening. Comparing across 2–3 jewellers. Sensitive to price, weight, BIS hallmark, and returnability. Sceptical of online jewellery.
Wants: confidence, comparison, a sense of how it will look on them.
The jeweller
A boutique to mid-size house. May be photographer, owner, or shop manager. Comfortable with Instagram and basic spreadsheets; not necessarily comfortable with web admin tools. Uploads in batches, calibrates try-on once per SKU.
Wants: a fast catalogue, a try-on that actually fits, proof it is working.
∞
Posture & voice
Editorial, restrained, confident. Copy reads like a knowledgeable showroom assistant — not a marketplace, not a luxury blog. Numbers (carat, weight, gold purity) are foregrounded. Adjectives are rationed.
Engineering posture
Static-first for catalogue; client-side for personalisation. Camera, MediaPipe inference, comparison, saves, and quiz answers all execute in the browser. No server round-trip should sit between a shopper and a piece of jewellery.

A note on slugs
Product slugs are human-readable (kundan-polki-choker-anaya) and immutable once published; canonical for SEO. Jeweller slugs (kasturi-jewellers-jaipur) include city to disambiguate. Collection & occasion slugs are short and timeless (monsoon-edit, bridal).

Touch rules
Hit targets ≥ 44 × 44 px, always
Bottom-half placement for primary CTAs on phones
Thumb-zone aware (right-hand bias is fine; we mirror nothing automatically)
No hover-only affordances; long-press is the supported equivalent
Safe-area insets honoured for notched devices
Type & density
Body text never below 15 px on mobile
Headlines reflow, not just shrink (e.g. PDP name moves above gallery)
Specs stay legible — never collapsed into a marquee or accordion alone
Try-on UI is always one-thumb operable; secondary controls are revealed, not hidden
Network & weight
Target: first meaningful paint < 1.5 s on a mid-range Android on 4G. Catalogue images served as AVIF with WebP fallback, lazy-loaded, sized to viewport. Try-on bundle (MediaPipe + overlay renderer) is route-split — never paid for outside /try-on and the embedded modal.

§ 09  ·  Principles
Premium jewellery retail,
translated for the screen.
Twelve rules — half drawn from showroom craft, half from web product instinct. When two designs both work, the one that better matches these wins.
i
The product, then everything else.
Imagery and specs always lead. Marketing copy, badges, and promos must earn the right to interrupt them.
— Showroom: the case, then the conversation.
ii
Restraint is the look.
Negative space, hairline rules, type-led layouts. Decoration is the jewellery itself; the page is the velvet.
— Less chrome, more chroma.
iii
Numbers are foregrounded.
Weight (g), purity (KT), gem weight (ct), price (₹) — never compressed into a paragraph. They are the contract.
— Specs are not fine print.
iv
Trust is structural, not decorative.
Hallmark, returns, certification — visible at the moment of doubt (PDP, footer of specs, near CTAs), not banished to a footer.
— Doubt is timed; reassurance must be too.
v
The jeweller is a person, not a brand block.
Every product carries its house. Provenance is part of the price.
— A name, a city, a face if possible.
vi
Imagery is editorial, not marketplace.
No watermark grids of identical white-background SKUs. Curated shoots; the catalogue tolerates difference.
— Closer to a magazine than to a marketplace.
vii
Quiet motion, never showy.

§ 11  ·  Trust
The seals we carry,
and where they sit.
A short list of trust signals built into V1. Each has a defined placement, a written treatment, and a clear non-decorative role.
BIS
BIS Hallmark
Every gold piece carries the BIS hallmark with HUID. Shown on PDP next to weight; jeweller’s BIS licence number shown on storefront.
/catalog/[slug] · /store/[jeweller] · ProductCard dot
CERT
Certified gemstones
Diamonds and coloured stones above a threshold carry a recognised lab certificate (IGI, GIA, SSEF). Cert number printed next to gem weight.
/catalog/[slug] · /compare
RET
Returns & buyback
Per-jeweller return window (typically 7 days) plus lifetime buyback policy where supported. Stated in plain language at the CTA.
/catalog/[slug] (sticky) · /help · /store/[jeweller]
22K
Purity, declared
Purity (14/18/22/24K) shown explicitly with hallmark match. Conflicting purity vs. hallmark is a blocking validation in upload.
/catalog/[slug] · ProductCard · /jeweller/products/new
₹
Transparent INR pricing
Price shown in INR with making charges noted separately. Live gold rate tooltip explains daily fluctuation.
/catalog/[slug] · /compare · ProductCard
SEC
Browser-only processing
Camera frames for try-on never leave the device. Stated on the permission overlay and in the persistent try-on hint.
/try-on · /search/image · /about
YR
Years in trade
Each jeweller shows its year of founding. A small but meaningful Indian retail proof point.
/store/[jeweller] · JewellerCard
SHIP
Insured shipping
Per-jeweller declared shipping & insurance policy, shown near the CTA at PDP and on storefront contact card.
/catalog/[slug] · /store/[jeweller]
CARE
Care & cleaning
Jewellery type-specific care notes (kundan, polki, gold, gem). Set expectations before purchase.
/catalog/[slug] (below specs) · /help
A note on placement
Trust signals appear near the moment of doubt, not in a banner at the top. The shopper’s questions are sequenced: is this real? (PDP hero), can I return it? (near CTA), can I trust the camera? (try-on overlay), is the price fair today? (price hover). Each seal is matched to the question it answers.
